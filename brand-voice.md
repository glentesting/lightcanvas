# LightCanvas Brand Voice

## The One-Sentence Version

LightCanvas sounds like a knowledgeable friend who does light shows — not a software company, not a startup, not a tutorial video.

---

## The Personality

**Confident.** LightCanvas knows what it is and doesn't apologize for it. No hedge words, no "you might want to consider," no unnecessary qualifiers. The app knows its job and does it.

**Direct.** Say the thing. Don't bury it in three sentences of setup. If a button does something, label it exactly what it does.

**Warm but not gushing.** We're not a bank. We're also not a golden retriever. Friendly, helpful, occasionally a little dry — never cold, never over-the-top enthusiastic.

**Respects the user's intelligence.** This community has been running complex multi-controller light shows for years. Don't explain what a channel is. Don't treat them like beginners unless they are. Trust them.

**Celebrates the craft.** These people are doing something genuinely impressive in their front yards. The app should reflect that — not in a cheesy way, but in a way that takes the work seriously.

---

## Tone by Context

### Onboarding / Empty States
Helpful and encouraging without being a cheerleader about it. Give them something to do, not something to read.

✅ "Add your first prop to get started."
✅ "Drop a song in and let's see what it sounds like."
❌ "Welcome to LightCanvas! We're so excited to have you here. Let's get started on your amazing light show journey!"

### Buttons and CTAs
Short. Action-first. No "Click here to..." — just the action.

✅ "Run AI Analysis"
✅ "Add Prop"
✅ "Upload Song"
✅ "Go Premium"
❌ "Click to Upload Your Song File"
❌ "Analyze with AI"
❌ "Upgrade Your Account"

### Error Messages
Honest, specific, not alarming. Tell them what happened and what to do next. Don't blame them, don't be vague, don't panic.

✅ "Couldn't load your display. Try refreshing — if it keeps happening, your session may have timed out."
✅ "That file type isn't supported. Upload an MP3 or WAV."
❌ "An unexpected error has occurred. Please try again later."
❌ "Oops! Something went wrong 😬"

### Success / Confirmation
Acknowledge it, move on. Don't celebrate minor actions.

✅ "Saved."
✅ "Song uploaded."
✅ "Sequence generated — review it in the Timeline."
❌ "Great job! Your song has been successfully uploaded to LightCanvas! 🎉"

### Upgrade / Paywall Prompts
Clear value, no guilt, one ask. Tell them exactly what they unlock, make it easy to say yes, make it easy to say not yet.

✅ "You've hit the free limit. Go Premium for unlimited controllers, songs, and sequences — $14.99/month or $129/year."
❌ "You've reached your limit! Upgrade now to unlock the full power of LightCanvas and take your show to the next level!"

### Tooltips and Helper Text
Short. Useful. Written like a knowledgeable person would say it, not like a manual.

✅ "Mega Tree — large triangle prop. Drag to place anywhere on your house."
✅ "AI Analysis reads the audio and maps beats, bass, and vocals to your props."
❌ "The Mega Tree prop is represented visually as a large triangle shape on the canvas and can be positioned anywhere on the house silhouette using click-and-drag functionality."

## Words and Phrases to Use

- "your display" (not "your project" or "your account")
- "props" (not "elements," "items," or "lights")
- "sequence" / "sequencing" (not "program" or "choreograph")
- "your show" (it's their show — own it)
- "house" (not "canvas background" or "template")
- "Run AI Analysis" (the exact label — don't vary it)
- "Go Premium" (not "Upgrade," not "Subscribe")

---

## Words and Phrases to Never Use

- "robust" — ever
- "seamless" — it's meaningless
- "intuitive" — if you have to say it, it isn't
- "powerful yet easy to use" — cliché, says nothing
- "unlock the full potential" — LinkedIn speak
- "synergy," "leverage," "holistic" — no
- "delight" as a verb ("we want to delight our users") — no
- "journey" — it's a light show app, not a wellness retreat
- Exclamation points on every sentence — pick your moments
- Emoji in UI copy — maybe very sparingly in marketing, never in the app itself
- Passive voice — "your sequence was generated" → "sequence generated"

---

## Writing Checklist

Before finalizing any copy, ask:

1. Is it shorter than it needs to be? If yes, good. If no, cut it.
2. Does it start with an action word or the key info? If not, reorder it.
3. Would a real person say this out loud? If not, rewrite it.
4. Does it assume the user is smart? It should.
5. Does it sound like any other SaaS app? If yes, make it sound like LightCanvas.

---

## The Vibe in One Line

The app should feel like it was built by someone who actually does light shows and got tired of the existing tools — because it was.
